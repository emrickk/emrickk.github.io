TRIGON is a bicycle brand that started out doing OEM work; nearly ten years ago it took "carbon fiber" as its point of breakthrough and set off down the road of building a bike brand of its own. What I'm showing off this time is TRIGON's entry-level carbon road bike, the TRIGON TR886.

The initial budget was around three thousand, then I happened to run into a colleague who seriously knows his bikes...ten thousand words omitted here...and in the end I decided to raise the budget just a tiny bit..to around eight thousand. The likes of Campagnolo and Bianchi are naturally out of reach for a riding newbie, and entirely unnecessary anyway, so I decided to start with an entry-level road bike. A 105 groupset. One nice thing about a complete bike from a small maker like TRIGON is that they use one groupset all the way through, unlike certain idiot manufacturers who stick a Tigra here and a sola there..As for feel, the 105's feedback is still a bit better than Sram Rival; Rival feels too stiff and clacks far too loudly. Also, it took me a few rides to discover that a road bike gives you nowhere to see which gear you are actually in..fine, I admit I'm slow![B004](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2020/02/B004.webp)The brakes.
