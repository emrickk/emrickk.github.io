Haven't written anything in ages. Today I'm paying off the whole backlog

Picking a hard drive is honestly a pretty simple job: whatever's cheap, buy it. But my dumbass genes had to act up, and I insisted on a LaCie. Expensive, for one, and when it arrived I found a scratch on it. Truly a faint, ball-aching sorrow. After the return I steeled myself, gritted my teeth, and bought a Seagate 4T desktop drive

![](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2020/02/T0w0THg0dEJNOTlyNUorQlB3V05kQzFlSmFEekoxOWt0UERtUldTK0pMTHJ2ZWQyUkZ5NzhnPT0.webp)

What I didn't expect when buying: by the iron law that gambling on low-probability events must end in failure, the drive I got ran with a strange noise and hit temperatures as high as 70 degrees

XXX's customer service would only recognize Seatools test results, insisting to my face that anything under 75 degrees is normal

Do I look like someone that easy to bamboozle!!

So, the pay-your-own-shipping return play (which means I got bamboozled anyway, didn't I)

Sigh. On the LaCie return, the 10 kuai I'd made off shipping insurance went right back out, plus 15 more of my own on freight (who told me to idiotically pay extra for SF Express)

That's right: Seagate was such a rip-off it gets no photo

Well, luckily money is not the problem. Next up I bought a bare 3T Seagate drive + an ORICO drive dock

And by the way, first time ever buying a bare drive on Tmall, damn. Nothing technically wrong with it, but I am mightily displeased

As for this ORICO dock, never have I seen a drive dock with build quality this awful, goddamn it!!

And it has a seven-color marquee light show, holy crap! Do I look like I'm into that twee aesthetic!! (facepalm

Alright, that's the end of the hard drive griping. I did also consider a 3T\*2 setup, but gave it up over value for money

The money's set aside for more important things

And that's that
