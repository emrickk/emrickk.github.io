One weekend in June 2024 we went to see the Egyptian museum in San Jose. It was closed. Through the palms we got the columns, striped like another continent's, and that was the whole visit.

![Striped columns behind palm fronds at the Egyptian museum](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/striped-columns-palm.webp)

The neighborhood around it was open. Gardens kept up, driveways swept, a Camry and a red Mustang out front, like dogs waiting for their owners.

  <img src="https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/red-mustang-garage.webp" alt="A red Mustang parked in front of wooden garage doors" loading="lazy" />
</div>

Our own car had nothing going on inside either, except the afternoon, which had let itself in and was lying on the leather.

![Leaf-dappled light on the brown leather seats of the car](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/m850-interior-dappled.webp)

The next day we drove north with the same amount of plan, and the afternoon went to Valencia Street.

![A couple with ice cream cones at a yellow-tiled corner on Valencia Street](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/valencia-ice-cream-corner.webp)

![The yellow Thomas D. Harvey Realtor corner building with vintage signs](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/valencia-harvey-realtor-corner.webp)

<div class="img-grid">
  <img src="https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/valencia-el-toro-crossing.webp" alt="Two people crossing at the taqueria corner" loading="lazy" />
  <img src="https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/valencia-caffe-vergnano-bench.webp" alt="Someone eating on a green bench outside the espresso bar, under an ice cream sign" loading="lazy" />

There was light left, so we kept going west until the street ended at the ocean.

The Camera Obscura was at its post on the terrace. The restaurant above the rocks has been closed for years. We looked in through the glass anyway: dust and a few loose screws on the counter, and every window still full of ocean.

![The Camera Obscura building on its terrace above Seal Rocks](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/giant-camera-seal-rocks.webp)

By eight the haze had gone pink. One figure had walked out to the very end of the rocks, waves coming in below.

<div class="img-grid">
  <img src="https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/wave-on-dark-sand.webp" alt="A wave spreading foam across dark sand" loading="lazy" />
  <img src="https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/figure-on-the-rock.webp" alt="A lone figure standing on the last rock above the ocean" loading="lazy" />
</div>

Two more sat on the warm side of the rocks, in jackets. Hey, not us. We watched the sunset out and shivered home.

![Two figures on the sunlit rocks above the waves at Sutro](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/sutro-rocks-two-figures.webp)
