In June 2024 we gave a weekend to no plan at all. The first day never left San Jose: we parked near the Rose Garden and walked around the Egyptian museum.

The museum announces itself from a block away. Behind the palms the columns turn striped. For a few blocks, California wears a different costume.

![Striped columns behind palm fronds at the Egyptian museum](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/striped-columns-palm.webp)

Around it, the Rose Garden neighborhood was having an ordinary weekend: swept driveways, cars waiting under the trees.

Ours waited in the shade too, the light coming through the leaves onto the seats.

![Leaf-dappled light on the brown leather seats of the car](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/m850-interior-dappled.webp)

The second day we decided, more or less on the spot, to drive north: to San Francisco, and to Valencia Street first.

On a June afternoon Valencia is all sun. At the corner of 21st, ice cream was being taken seriously.

![A couple with ice cream cones at a yellow-tiled corner on Valencia Street](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/valencia-ice-cream-corner.webp)

The street wears its signs in layers: Thomas D. Harvey, Realtor, twice over on a yellow corner, with a Retail For Lease notice hung underneath.

![The yellow Thomas D. Harvey Realtor corner building with vintage signs](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/valencia-harvey-realtor-corner.webp)

At the taqueria corner an old man crossed one way while a woman with a cane and a grocery bag went the other. Outside the espresso bar, under a neon ice cream cone, somebody sat eating on the green bench, scooter parked at hand.

There was still light left, so we kept going west until the streets ran out. At Lands End the little building that is a camera was still on its terrace, aimed at Seal Rocks.

![The Camera Obscura building on its terrace above Seal Rocks](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/giant-camera-seal-rocks.webp)

The old restaurant above the rocks has been dark for years; through the glass, the counter is down to dust and a few loose screws.

<div class="img-grid">
  <img src="https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/lands-end-closed-diner.webp" alt="The dark dining room of the closed restaurant, sea light in the windows" loading="lazy" />
  <img src="https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/seal-rocks-pink-haze.webp" alt="Seal Rocks under a pink evening haze" loading="lazy" />
</div>

By eight the haze had turned pink. Below, the waves kept unloading on the dark sand, and out on the last rock somebody stood with the whole Pacific to themselves.

<div class="img-grid">
  <img src="https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/wave-on-dark-sand.webp" alt="A wave spreading foam across dark sand" loading="lazy" />
  <img src="https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/figure-on-the-rock.webp" alt="A lone figure standing on the last rock above the ocean" loading="lazy" />
</div>

The sun went down at its own pace. Two people had climbed out to the warm side of the rocks to see it through.

![Two figures on the sunlit rocks above the waves at Sutro](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/sutro-rocks-two-figures.webp)

That was the whole weekend: no plan, two days, and a drive that stopped only because the land did.
