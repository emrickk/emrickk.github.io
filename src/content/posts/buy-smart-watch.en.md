I had been wanting to buy a smartwatch for ages, but with the big brands' so-called smartwatches priced perpetually sky-high, I could never harden my heart to buy one (well, who told me to be poor

The first time I wanted one of these things was when I saw the Smart Watch from dear Sony (which at the time, I think, was still called Sony Ericsson). Back then the so-called smartwatch was merely a second display for your phone (as if that weren't still the case now..), but loving these little gadgets as deeply as I do, I was drawn in all the same.

Later moto put out another little watch, the actv8 I think, and it set my heart itching like crazy again. And thinking of the recently released moto360, it seems the old houses really are the stronger hands at this kind of industrial design

Alright, back to the point. Why did I buy this post's protagonist.. because JD had it on special at 398, so I picked up the Galaxy Gear V700. This watch is actually great fun to tinker with; the specs are said to be a frequency-locked 1.6g dual-core TI processor plus an AMOLED screen. The display is quite crisp, and it runs smoothly. It even throws in a 2-megapixel camera; for four hundred kuai, the value is simply off the charts.

After a few days of play it ended up shelved all the same. Let me note a few points to mind

1 I recommend flashing the null23 system for the experience. After null23 the watch becomes a standalone device and can do many things on its own. Most importantly, it saves battery.

2 Tizen works very nicely with a Samsung phone, but once flashed it is not easy to flash back to Android. Back then it took enormous effort.. to rescue it from the plight of a dead touchscreen after flashing back to Android

---All in all, for a smartwatch priced at a mere four hundred yuan, everything it should have and everything it shouldn't, it has. If you are a phone junkie, I believe it will bring you surprises. Incidentally, I've spotted Samsung putting out yet another wearable with a curved screen, and my heart is itching like crazy once more
