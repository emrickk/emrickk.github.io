October is San Francisco's real summer: the fog takes a few weeks off, and the city remembers it lives on an ocean. One clear evening in October 2024 we went out to Lands End, the corner where the city plainly runs out of land, to do what everyone goes there to do: see the sun off.

We started at a window table above the rocks, and stayed a little longer than the food required.

![A window table in a wood-paneled dining room, the Pacific outside](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/lands-end-diner-window.webp)

## The plaza

Behind the Cliff House sits the Camera Obscura, a building shaped like a camera that has been pointed at the same rocks since 1946. The plaza in front of it belonged entirely to one kid on a scooter, doing laps.

![A kid on a scooter crossing the empty plaza in front of the camera-shaped Camera Obscura building](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/lands-end-camera-obscura.webp)

![An older couple walking under the white terrace arch toward the sea haze](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/lands-end-terrace-couple.webp)

## The ruins

Down the stairs, the concrete outlines in the cove are what is left of Sutro Baths: seven pools under a glass roof in 1896, billed as the largest in the world, burned down in 1966. The ocean has been taking the site back ever since. These days the walls hold graffiti, and kids climb where the divers went in.

The Cliff House above it is the third or fourth Cliff House, depending on how you count. This spot has burned down often enough that the current building still feels provisional.

![The white Cliff House building on the cliff edge above the surf](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/lands-end-cliff-house.webp)

## The trail

The Coastal Trail runs north from the baths through cypress that the wind has combed flat. Coves open up below, Marin sits across the water, and at one bend the Golden Gate shows up between the trees, red as ever.

## The cliff

An hour before sunset the cliff edge starts to fill. People pick a spot, sit down in the dust, and gradually stop talking. Below them, Seal Rocks: the sea lions moved to Pier 39 decades ago, but nobody has updated the name.

## After

The sun went down behind Seal Rocks and nobody left. The sky kept working for another half hour: orange, then pink, then one thin stripe of light lying on the horizon.

The whole show is free, which in this city counts as a small miracle.
