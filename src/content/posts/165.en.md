title: 'What Honesty Means'
---

Most of the time, the places where a person shines take time and care to notice. Summing it up: no matter someone's gender, age, profession, dress, manner of speaking, or personality, what I admire most is almost always honesty. Even when that honesty exposes the person's fragility, cowardice, vanity, childishness, dullness and so on, it never makes me think less of them; if anything, it may let me glimpse some fresh and lovable innocence. Seeing these things also lets me look at the world with more richness and tenderness.

A person's virtues can hardly exist on their own; the reverse side is usually a flaw. Take my kind of tenderness, for example: seen from a certain angle it is also a kind of distance. It is hard to judge a person by things that almost always have two sides. So what honesty means is accepting your true self, and, because of that, not being harsh with others. This point matters enormously, whether I am looking at others or at myself.
