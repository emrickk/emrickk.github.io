First, let's talk about why there needs to be a Lanxin at all.

Rumor on the street has it that our humble digits company, being one of the strongest contenders for the fourth big-tech seat after BAT, could hardly project big-league cachet while conducting its business over QQ all year round. Add the storied 3Q War of years past, which in one stroke dragged the digits up onto the big-league stage, and using anything from the Goose Factory was naturally out of the question forever after.

Of course, that is all my own nonsense. The so-called fourth big tech is about as meaningful as the contest over which is China's third-ranked university after Tsinghua and Peking University. In any case, one way or another, Lanxin came into being, succeeding Whatsapp and Fetion as our company's official communication tool.

As for Lanxin's ordinary functions as an IM, I'll spare the words; after all, when sending files everyone still uses the cloud drive, and after sending an important message we still ping the other person on WeChat to remind them to go check Lanxin. As the court-appointed IM of the digits company, its signature features go like this...

* Departure notices.. after all, there isn't much other news all day long anyway

![lanxin-pic2](https://cdn.anping.us/2020/02/lanxin-pic2.webp)

* File transfer alerts.. after all, nine times out of ten a file transfer is going to fail... so when you see a file coming in, just ask what it is and have it sent to the cloud drive..
* A demo of a meeting check-in feature... why a demo... because who the hell would use something this dumb!
