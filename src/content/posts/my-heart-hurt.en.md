title: 'Seven Times My Heart Has Grieved'
---

The first time, when it could have pressed forward, yet it feigned humility;

The second time, when it was empty, it filled itself with lust and longing;

The third time, between the hard and the easy, it chose the easy;

The fourth time, it did wrong, yet consoled itself that others do wrong as well;

The fifth time, it was weak by its own choice, yet took that weakness for the tenacity of life;

The sixth time, when it scorned an ugly face, not knowing that face was one of its own masks;

The seventh time, it settled into the mire of life; not resigned to it, yet cowering all the same.

-----------------------------------------------------------------

The happy life I can imagine

Has no long stretch of soundless dark

Days joyful and wide awake, nights of quiet sleep

At every moment currents of heart being exchanged

No one anywhere enduring loneliness

Living in such a country

Even without boundless coastlines, forests or fields of wheat

There are gardens everywhere all the same

People tear off their thousand masks and coverings

And laugh with abandon

The streets everywhere are full of warm-hearted souls

Not scarce at all, verging on overflowing

Everyone caring for everyone

A gaze and a smile in passing

What is it, to be on one's guard?

No one knows

My family, living the life closest to life

Safe meals, sturdy shelter

No watching for danger, no worry of illness

Doors wide open, roads running clear, a park right at the window

Children can see rainbows, dragonflies and dandelions

Cars slow down for them

Teachers teach them freedom

Even losing the way alone at night, no one goes missing

The road home can always be found

Home is always there, it never moves away

Never flustered

Under any circumstances

Never flustered

Life has never once produced the situation called:

"I have so many things I need to do,

but I don't know what comes next"

No trembling unease over any standstill

Never comparing

The beloved is everlasting, blazing, deep in devotion

Found at first seeking, hand in hand into old age

He draws near, and you know he understands you

He goes away, and you know he misses you

With a silent gaze, or with sweet words

A long embrace, or the light brush of lips

Keeping two hearts close, and love at its constant warmth, always

There is little that money can buy

For spending cannot produce a lasting joy

That momentary rapture

Always trailed by the letdown of a wish too easily granted

Holds no appeal whatsoever

No one cares for it

What we call faith is not idol worship in any form

But one creed of values and of conduct held in common

All things bending toward the good

Everyone carries a dream born with them

Written on their identity papers

Never to be altered by the opinions of others

The wellspring of all excitement and all striving

The greatest joy is to witness a dream come true

The greatest evil is to keep another from reaching theirs

The bastards who block a dream's fulfillment receive the harshest punishment

Sentenced to the destruction of their own dream

The equal of being denied a good death

Everyone, in the end, fulfills the dream they were born with

Just as everyone, in the end, must die

It is a part that belongs to the closing of a life

So that the long sleep of all the kind-hearted

Is held in a happiness beyond reproach
