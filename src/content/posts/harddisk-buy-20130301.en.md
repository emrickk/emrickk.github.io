Sold my old 2TB Seagate GoFlex to EJJ, so I started planning to buy a new 3TB drive.

Initially I settled on two options to choose between.

1.A bare 3TB Seagate drive + an ORICO drive dock

2.Just buy a 3.5-inch desktop drive outright

The bare drive + dock option felt a bit better; after all, upgrading or swapping it out later would be more convenient.

For the drive I still planned to go with Seagate's 1TB-per-platter 3TB option, that is, the Seagate ST3000DM001; 1TB per platter runs a bit faster.

For the enclosure, ORICO again; my initial pick was the 7619US3, passable performance and decent looks too.

I still don't much like those toaster-style docks; leaving the drive exposed like that is just too brutal.

Then I opened Taobao, typed in 3T 3.5, and saw the LaCie Minimus 3TB.

I was instantly won over by the look of it, and at only 1,098 yuan it was a real bargain; purchase complete.
