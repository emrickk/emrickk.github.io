I lived in San Diego for three years. I have moved away since, but I still go back from time to time, and every trip starts the same way: the minute I step out of the airport, the smell of San Diego. I could not tell you what it is, and I have never tried to find out. I just like it. When the smell shows up, I know I have arrived.

Same again this time: the Alila in Carlsbad, up on the bluff. The geography here is simple, a lagoon on one side, the beach on the other, the 101 threading between them, and most of what the town does happens between those three things. Sit by the room window for a while and it files past: pelicans in a line along the bluff, cars on the highway heading for the beach, people on the beach heading for the water. What this town is about, you can pretty much work out from one window.

One day we drove into the city. Passing the street where I lived for three years, I stopped the car; there was nothing to do there, I just wanted to walk it once more. It is still nice. Then Balboa Park.

![The street I lived on for three years](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/the-old-street.webp)

![Kids at the big fountain in Balboa Park](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/balboa-park-fountain.webp)

In 2023, when I was looking for a new job, the gliderport was one of my favorite places: drive over in the evening, watch the sun go down from the cliff, sometimes take the little climb partway down. This time it was cloudy, so there was no sunset to wait for. People walked out to the edge and looked at the silver water anyway. I did too. I still like it.
