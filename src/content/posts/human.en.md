title: 'The Mature Person'
description: 'A note on maturity: separating yourself from others, keeping clear boundaries, and staying close to family without losing yourself.'
---

A mature person can distinguish, and accept, that they and other people are separate individuals; they can draw clear boundaries around the self, hold a sound concept of who they are, and build good, meaningful relationships with their own family system, without becoming so absorbed in their family's lives that they lose themselves. This means they can use their emotions freely: they are not brimming with resentment, nor do they love anyone too desperately, and they can hold steady when steadiness is called for.
