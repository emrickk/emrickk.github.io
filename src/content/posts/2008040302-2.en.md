Original post: [5 Steps to Overcome Fear of Failure](http://www.abetteryoublog.com/2007/02/27/5-steps-to-overcome-fear-of-failure/)

Translated by: [闲人莫入](http://angelived.org/)

Fear is like a fog that rolls in without warning: it narrows your vision and clouds your reasoning, and when you find you can no longer tell goal from direction, can you find a way out? Has worry made you retreat? Do you know how to keep moving forward? Fear of failure touches everyone. Once you recognize it in your own life, you can follow the 5 steps below to understand it, resolve it, and finally reach your goals.

One. **Recognize the fear**. What do you fear most? Have you worried that your company might go under? Have you worried that you will never find that special person in your life, or that you will lose the ones you love? Have you ever felt your thoughts flooded by your own worries? In which areas of life has the fear of failure kept you from concentrating and so kept success out of reach? Pray, think, then listen. Let God show you where your worries lie, and then consider what to do to be released from them.

Fear of failure may show itself like this:

1. **Procrastination**. Do you have a habit of putting things off to the last minute? That way, perfectionist that you are, you have a ready excuse for any failure; but in doing so, you have created, out of nothing, precisely the failure you hoped to avoid.
2. **Paralysis**. Do you feel crushed breathless by your own insecurity and uncertainty? Are you letting fear knock you down completely, brushing past opportunities that come once in a thousand years? Anxiety about success is really the fear of the failure that might follow it.
3. **Overreaction**. Are you irritable, closed-off, or aggressive? If you fear failure, you may find yourself reacting far more strongly than you once did to similar situations. Is it your fear driving these reactions, rather than letting you face life calmly?
4. **Wallowing**. Do you relieve your stress by hurting yourself? Have you already gone numb to the fear, because you can find no way at all to overcome it?

Two. **Size up the fear**. If you are standing on crumbling ground at a cliff's edge, fear makes your body release adrenaline to warn you that danger is present. You can use that fear to step back onto safe ground, or you can ignore it entirely, in which case the likely outcome is, of course, falling. Once you have lost your footing, no amount of positive thinking in the world can save you. Before you take these 5 steps and press on, first make sure you truly need to overcome this fear. Fear is an alarm your body sounds for you, but it can also go off when it isn't needed. Is God using fear to hint that your choice is dangerous, or should you press forward regardless, break through, and reach your goal at last? Life experience can make you overly cautious, and a fear of risk may cost you golden opportunities. Life requires weighing things up; if you have decided you are going to walk out of your fear, then you can.

To focus your mind on the positive and overcome the fear, you must first understand it. **What would the worst case look like?** Write down everything a failure would mean; looking at your answers makes things more concrete. If the end of the world came, would any of this be worth mentioning? Would your family be ruined? Would your reputation be swept away? Do you worry that failure would bring every one of these misfortunes to pass, one by one? Do you think that once you fail, you are worth nothing? Record these messages that your fear of failure is sending you.

Three. **Reinterpret**. Once you have received the messages your fear brings, reframe them with positive thoughts. You may fail, but with each failure you draw closer to success. Your identity is not changed by failure after failure; you will become what you set out to become. **Face the fear, and create success**. You can draw support from those who encourage you, but you must still be your own head cheerleader.

Fear is your friend. When it reminds you that a decision works against your life's goals, it can help you change your approach and spare your life from regret. And if it means to pile obstacle after obstacle across your road to success, then it is giving you the chance to keep improving, to walk out of the past, and to become a better person. The secret to living free of fear's grip is to embrace it.

I started my first business in an unfamiliar state, with no business partner. In front of other professionals I appeared full of confidence, but inside I was full of worry. From the very beginning I made a decision: let God carry my worries away. I firmly believed that coming here was the right choice, that I had a sound business plan, and that I had the ability to steer toward success, so I did not worry about the things I could not control. In practice, this meant living off credit card overdrafts for the first few months, but I built up my client base, and as my reputation grew, my company grew fast; before long I quit my old job. Fear might have kept me from ever striking out on my own, and had I let it fester, I might still be at my old company today. If others had discovered that I lacked confidence in my own abilities, they would have lost confidence in me too. So I chose to answer positively with positive thinking, to overcome it with everything I had, and I won.

Four. **Strip it of its power over me**. Your past shapes how you perceive and understand the present. As if through a magnifying glass, dangers look far larger than they really are, because you are trapped in memories of past failures. Remove these uncertainties and the hold the past has on you, and work to cut down its strength. Wounds, losses, and painful memories all color your view of reality; even the smallest thing can push you into irrational behavior in daily life. Along the road of your exploration, fear will also grow out of all proportion; try to shrink it back to its true size.

I remember stepping barefoot into a swarm of red ants when I was a child; the pain seemed as if it would last forever, along with the sadness of missing the fun of camp that day while my foot was treated. Later, when I had children of my own, I found myself forever on guard against ants, forever worrying about where my next step would land. It was absurd, but I never even noticed I was doing it, until one day I saw my own children scanning the ground for ants too. I stopped short: so they did it as well. I made a conscious decision to change this fear, reminding myself again and again that my once-in-a-century misfortune was unlikely to repeat itself. So now when the children and I play outdoors, we simply play to our hearts' content; if we spot a swarm of ants, we steer clear, but it is the last thing on our minds. Life is too short to be spent on dread, and once you can think that way, fear crumbles at a touch.

Five. **Use the fear**. Have you ever lost something because fear stood in your way? Do not turn angry or dejected; what you should do is spur yourself toward the successes ahead. Are you afraid to dream because of the obstacles you might face? Overcome the fear; there are great things only you can accomplish, so act on them now!
