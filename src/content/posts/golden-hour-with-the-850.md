One Saturday at the beginning of August 2024, I spent the day in San Francisco with the camera: cable cars, cafe windows, the crowds along Market Street. But the frames I was really saving the light for came in the evening, back in the South Bay. On summer weekends the office-park lots there empty out completely, which turns them into the quietest photo studios you could ask for. The model that evening was the 850.

![The 850 in profile across the empty lot](https://cdn.anping.us/2026/07/bmw-850-profile-afternoon.webp)

We started in the shade at the edge of the lot, where the trees soften everything and the paint reads as a quiet silver-grey. The Gran Coupe roofline does its best work in a shot like this: one long line from the nose all the way down into the tail.

![Front three-quarter view in the tree shade](https://cdn.anping.us/2026/07/bmw-850-front-shade.webp)

Up close it turns out the front end is all texture: the blacked-out kidney grille with the M badge tucked into the corner, and the laser headlights that look less like lamps and more like something out of a watch case.

Then the sun dropped below the tree line and everything changed. The same paint that had been reading silver all evening suddenly went warm, and every panel picked up its own version of the sunset.

![Front three-quarter view in golden light](https://cdn.anping.us/2026/07/bmw-850-front-golden.webp)

![Head-on, staring straight into the camera](https://cdn.anping.us/2026/07/bmw-850-head-on.webp)

The low sun came straight through the spokes of the wheels and laid the whole side of the car out in gold. This might be my favorite frame of the evening.

![Side profile with the sun flaring through the wheels](https://cdn.anping.us/2026/07/bmw-850-side-pano.webp)

The rear deserves its own study. The taillights stretch into the haunches, and the M850i badge sits just above the diffuser like a signature in the corner of the canvas.

Wide shots for scale: an entire parking lot, one car, nobody else around. The white COMPACT stencils on the asphalt are the only audience.

As the light went out the shots got darker and moodier, which suits this car just as well as the gold did.

![Rear three-quarter view in the fading light](https://cdn.anping.us/2026/07/bmw-850-rear-moody.webp)

![The dashboard glowing at dusk](https://cdn.anping.us/2026/07/bmw-850-interior-dusk.webp)

And one last portrait before heading home, of the other member of the crew: the red panda who rides along on the rear window, peeking over the door the whole way.

![The red panda sticker peeking over the window](https://cdn.anping.us/2026/07/bmw-850-red-panda.webp)

An empty lot, one car, one golden hour. Some evenings that is all it takes.
