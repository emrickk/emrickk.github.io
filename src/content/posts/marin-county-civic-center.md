Last July we were driving north from San Francisco toward [the redwoods](/posts/two-days-in-the-redwoods/). Before we reached the trees, we took a ten-minute detour off Highway 101 in San Rafael to visit Frank Lloyd Wright's Marin County Civic Center.

Wright began designing it in 1957, at ninety. It was his last commission. He died in the spring of 1959, before ground was broken, and the county spent the sixties finishing it without him: two long wings riding from hilltop to hilltop, a sky-blue roof, a gold spire, and a skylight running the entire length of each wing. It is still a working government building.

![The gold anodized gates of the civic center lobby, a woman waiting on a bench in the sun](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/civic-center-gold-gates.webp)

## Corridors with sky in them

The building has almost no interior in the usual sense. The hallways are balconies, the balconies look down into gardens, and above them runs one long window of sky. The escalator carries you out of the shade toward the glass. None of it looks sixty years old, which helps explain why Gattaca came here when it needed a ready-made future.

And it is pink. Nobody quite warns you about that. The walls are salmon, the floors are red terrazzo polished until they hold the sky, and the window frames, gates and rails are all gold. On a July afternoon the fourth floor glowed like the inside of a seashell.

![The curved balcony corridor on the fourth floor, salmon walls glowing under the skylight](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/civic-center-curved-corridor.webp)

## The garden inside

Down the middle of each atrium runs a planter. In it grows, not to put too fine a point on it, a jungle: schefflera, birds of paradise, philodendron and palms, all leaning toward the light.

## The library

We ended the visit in the library at the top of the building: a round desk, a circular leather bench, red walls, and afternoon light falling through the skylight. If nobody reminded you, it would be easy to forget that you were inside a county government building.

![The library's round desk and circular bench under the skylight](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/civic-center-library-desk.webp)

The building opened in 1962 and looks as if it opened last month: the terrazzo is polished, the gold metalwork is bright, the plants are thriving, and the skylights are clean. For more than sixty years, people have polished the floors, repaired the building, cleaned the skylights and watered the plants. Year after year, the county has made room for that work in the budget. That was what we talked about for the rest of the drive north.
