An hour up the Mississippi from New Orleans sits Oak Alley, the sugar plantation from the postcards. From a distance I took a first shot of the big house. I turned around, and there was a sign right beside me: I had been standing next to the rebuilt cabins, where the enslaved lived.

![The big house at the end of a path, framed in dark oak branches](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/plantation-house-under-oaks.webp)

Then I walked in and took another. I did not think the house was that beautiful anymore.

![The big house at Oak Alley, columns wrapping the gallery, a magnolia at the corner](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/plantation-house-front.webp)
