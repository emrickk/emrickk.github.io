description: 'A passage from Qian Zhongshu''s "Fortress Besieged" about the two kinds of people who eat a bunch of grapes.'

There are only two kinds of people in the world. Say a bunch of grapes comes into your hands: one kind picks the best ones and eats those first, the other saves the best for last. By rights the first kind ought to be optimists, since every grape they eat is the best of what remains; the second kind ought to be pessimists, since every grape they eat is the worst of what remains. Yet in fact it is just the opposite, and the reason is that the second kind still has hope, while the first has only memories. (Qian Zhongshu)
