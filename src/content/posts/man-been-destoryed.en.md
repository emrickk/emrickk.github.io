description: 'A passage from the opening of Alfred Bester''s The Demolished Man, on coincidences replayed again and again across the universe.'

In the endless universe everything follows the old tracks; nothing differs, nothing is new. Humanity takes itself for a miracle of vast upheaval, yet under the great eye of God it is merely an ordinary thing. This strange instant in a life, this extraordinary event, all the astonishing coincidences of circumstance, of chance, of accident… will be played out again and again, on some planet in some galaxy.
