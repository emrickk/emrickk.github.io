Once there was a rich man who loved hearing jokes, and whoever made him laugh was rewarded with gold. The more jokes the rich man heard, the harder it became for him to laugh with any pleasure. A poor scholar, pressed to make a living, went to tell jokes too. That day he passed along the long gallery of carved dragons and painted beams and came into the resplendent main hall of the mansion, where the rich man and his eminent guests sat solemn and upright. The poor scholar dropped to his knees with a thud and spoke a single line that sent everyone present toppling over with laughter. He said: we poor people have our dignity too
