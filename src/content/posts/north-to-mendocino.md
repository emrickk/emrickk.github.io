The trip came out of dragging Google Maps around after dinner: a name three hours north, on the water, that neither of us knew much about. The planning ended there.

We left early Saturday morning, and the coast changed temper the farther north we drove. Past the Golden Gate the beaches and the wind's patience run out together, and by the little town of Albion there was nothing left but cliff, swell, and wind-flattened grass.

## The cove

We stayed at a small inn on the headland at Albion; below the lawn outside the room, a river reaches the sea under the last wooden bridge on Highway 1.

The bridge is wartime carpentry. In 1944 the steel clearly had somewhere more important to be, so the bridge had to make do with wood. Luckily, good wood is one thing California has never been short of. We had crossed it ourselves on the way in.

![The wooden trestle bridge over the Albion River mouth](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/albion-river-bridge.webp)

The lawn runs out at a split-rail fence, and the fence is the whole show: past it the ground lets go and the cove takes over. I stood there the better part of an afternoon, watching the same view run small variations on itself, the swell working the sea stack, the pampas grass catching the light and letting it go. I know these pictures look surprisingly alike. Come watch the sea with me anyway 🐶

## Point Cabrillo

Point Cabrillo is a lighthouse nearby. You park beside the keeper's old houses, an inn themselves these days, and walk the last half mile on your own: downhill, fences on both sides, the lighthouse growing slowly at the end of the path.

![Walking the path down to the light station](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/point-cabrillo-walk-in.webp)

![The path past the keeper's houses in the cypress](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/point-cabrillo-path.webp)

To be honest, Point Cabrillo is not really a lighthouse. It is more of a small village: keeper's houses in the cypress, an oil house with a red roof, white picket fences. The house at the end of the grass that grew a lantern is the most conspicuous thing there, and somehow still not the main character.

The visitor center and gift shop got browsed, of course. The little bookstore is a careful one, stocked with souvenirs so fine you hold your breath around them. And past the glass, the real Pacific roared on with its afternoon.

Still, the sea can rage all it likes; the whole point of a lighthouse is to get along with it. And with the sun, a rare guest this late in the year, stepping in to mediate, we came away with some very presentable, very artistic colors.

## The road home

Just past three on Sunday the car pointed south again. Two days of coastal bends and not one complaint; before the drive home it got a pullout above the surf to itself.

![The car parked on a pullout above the surf](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/car-on-the-headland.webp)

Then the road tipped uphill one more time and took us with it. Home!

![The car climbing the road out](https://cdn.theneverless.com/2026/07/car-up-the-road.webp)
