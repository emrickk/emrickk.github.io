> If you pronounce BMW out loud your hear “Bee Em Double You”. As with many things we like and I guess dislike, we tend to give cars shorter and easier to pronounce names, kind of to bring them closer to you and make them your own. So owners all over the world started giving these shortened personal names to the cars “Bimmers”.

**Getting Ready to Buy a Car**

A while back, after graduating, I moved from Boston to Southern California, and buying a car went straight onto the agenda. Boston has convenient public transit and the city is small, so getting anywhere meant either the subway or walking - but in SoCal, no car means no legs =.=  
Without a car nothing is convenient, and with the recent pandemic hardly anyone is driving for Uber or Lyft either, so I urgently needed a car.

**Choosing the Car**

Since the main use is just getting around, I honestly don't have any especially high requirements for the car:

* Has to be a compact sedan. I used to drive the family XT5, and a big car is honestly just torture for a rookie like me..

- Power shouldn't be too weak. Not a fan of the feeling where you floor the gas and nothing happens..

- Comfortable to drive. Very subjective, this one lol

- Must have Carplay/lane keeping/lane change assist/Auto Hold

- Fuel economy can't be too bad

- In stock. If it's not in stock, who knows what year of the monkey it might arrive.. and yet yours truly still fell into a hole here, more on that later

Plus I was planning to lease (basically a long-term rental) rather than buy, so it only needs to be reliable for the first three years.

My monthly lease budget was under 450, and after looking around, the candidates were roughly these:

* BMW 430i/330i

- MB CLA

- Audi A3/A4/A5

I originally wanted to consider the Lexus IS350 too, but one look at the interior, truly ugly, plus lease terms far too unfriendly with almost no discounts. Pass…

Went around looking at Audis: the A3 felt too small, the A4 has a four in the name which I don't want lol, and the A5 sportback went over budget once optioned out.. pass  
Test drove the CLA, and honestly, apart from the space being a bit small (really small..) nothing else felt wrong. But being a new model it had basically no discounts, and after a lap of the SoCal dealers, all the in-stock cars were high-trim units piled with useless options. Over budget, pass  
The BMW 430i is also very nice: enough power, frameless doors, basically all the tech features. But dealer inventory was thin everywhere, and even now that the new model is out, the discounts on the old one still aren't much.. inventory cars were basically 50k+ MSRP.. over budget + no desire to buy an outgoing model without a real discount, pass  
Finally I looked at the 330i. The 330i counts as the safe pick, I suppose.. freshly redesigned, power not weak, plenty of tech, ample in-stock inventory with rich configuration choices, and when I test drove a friend's car before, it felt quite comfortable to drive. And the key point: even though pandemic discounts lately aren't what they usually are, they still aren't small! This is the one!

**Choosing the Options**

My strategy was to ignore each dealer's inventory at first and just pick the configuration I wanted, then see which dealer had stock that best matched it.  
Plus I needed an in-stock car, so exterior customization was basically off the table.. all I could do was lock in the options and leave the inventory to fate

Ideally it would have these options:

* Convenience Package. It only adds 2350, but includes lane change assist, blind spot warning, keyless entry, LED headlights, lumbar support, and park distance control. An absolute must-pick

- Live Cockpit Professional. Upgrades the instrument cluster to a 10-inch digital screen, the center display to 12 inches, and iDrive to 7.0 (yes, the 3 Series in North America still comes standard with 6.0.. what a move)

- Harman Kardon sound system

- Ambient lighting. It only adds 250… but the mood factor is maxed out

- Power trunk. Super convenient, no explanation needed.. and it also only adds 250

- Interior preferably cognac (something like a caramel color)

But after a final sweep of the inventory, in-stock cars fitted with the Harman Kardon sound system were extremely rare… and ones where a good discount could be negotiated were rarer still..  
There was one car that had every option I wanted, and a cognac interior to boot. But it was also fitted with a pile of other options, nearly everything selectable was selected, the M package was on it too, and the MSRP shot straight to 59k… with that kind of money, why wouldn't I just buy an M340i =.=

In the end I picked a white-on-black one, fitted with the Convenience Package + Live Cockpit Professional + HK sound system. No ambient lighting or power trunk, but I lived with it..  
This car's MSRP was 46070, and the final negotiated price was 42000.  
The lease came out to 10k mileage/36 months, 0 down (zero down payment), Max MSDs (equivalent to a 3150-dollar refundable deposit), 2200 DAS (what you pay at pickup, including various taxes, fees, and the first month's payment), and 410+ a month. Call it a price where I neither lost nor gained

**Interlude**

At the time this car was parked at a port only 20 miles from the dealer, so it counted as half in stock. Deal done, I went home all pleased with myself to wait, figuring 7 or 10 days and it would surely arrive, but it took three weeks for the car to reach the store =.=  
Asked around and found out that because of COVID, truck drivers were making more on unemployment benefits than on the job, so they went on strike en masse, and with the labor shortage the schedules slipped across the board =.=  
Fine then..
