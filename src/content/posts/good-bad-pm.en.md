By Dai Yusen

Success/Failure

A good product manager pours everything into making the product succeed  
A bad product manager finds every excuse for why the product failed

Goals  
A good product manager spends most of their time on "what to build", and on managing how it gets built  
A bad product manager spends most of their time on the details of "how to build it"  
  
A good product manager focuses on what should be done  
A bad product manager focuses on what could be done  
  
A good product manager focuses on winning the competition  
A bad product manager focuses on getting things finished  
  
A good product manager thinks first about user scenarios  
A bad product manager thinks first about system logic  
  
A good product manager cares most about users and business goals  
A bad product manager cares most about what the competitors just did  
  
A good product manager defines what their own work and achievements are  
A bad product manager always hopes to be told what they should do  
  
A good product manager always knows what the next most important thing is  
A bad product manager easily gets new priorities shoved in, because the old priorities were never thought through  
  
A good product manager reaches consensus with the boss on priorities  
A bad product manager complains that the boss keeps dreaming up new requirements to squeeze in  
  
A good product manager focuses limited resources on the few things that most advance the product's goals  
A bad product manager writes blog posts analyzing whether buttons should really have borders  
  
A good product manager steams plain buns for the poor and lays out an imperial feast for the rich  
A bad product manager steams plain buns for the rich and lays out an imperial feast for the poor  
  
A good product manager sees first the hope that the product might succeed  
A bad product manager sees first the potential failures and difficulties  
  
  
Ways of working  
A good product manager foresees the problems that may arise and has solutions ready  
A bad product manager digs holes and spends every day busy filling them  
  
A good product manager, after making a mistake, builds rules and processes so the same mistake never happens again  
A bad product manager finishes filling one hole and moves on to fill the next  
  
A good product manager predicts, before launch, how the numbers might move, and instruments the points that will need analysis ahead of time  
A bad product manager is startled by the numbers after launch, and discovers the places needing deeper analysis were never instrumented.  
  
A good product manager says the data should look like this, because XX, XX and XXX  
A bad product manager says let's run an A/B test and see what the data does  
  
A good product manager sets the product's marketing direction and plan, and weaves them into the product design  
A bad product manager thinks marketing is entirely the marketing department's job  
  
A good product manager builds their own reputation and builds their own team  
A bad product manager complains the company won't hire people for them  
  
Communication  
A good product manager lets the boss clearly see the return on the resources invested  
A bad product manager complains the boss won't give them resources  
  
A good product manager can clearly define goals, decision processes and key resources in words and diagrams, and makes sure every party involved understands them correctly  
A bad product manager communicates casually by word of mouth, assuming everyone will naturally know what they are thinking  
  
A good product manager becomes a real working partner and friend to the related departments  
A bad product manager mostly stays inside their own department  
  
A good product manager builds an excellent product together with the engineering team  
A bad product manager leaves engineering with no idea what they are doing  
  
A good product manager persuades the designers to think in terms of the product's goals  
A bad product manager stands behind the designers, pointing and decreeing
