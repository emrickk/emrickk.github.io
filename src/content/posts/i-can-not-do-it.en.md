title: 'How Can It Be Done'
description: 'Ask "how can it be done" before "can it be done": only after every how is exhausted does "I can''t" become a fact.'
---

When something comes up, do not start by asking "can this be done"; start by asking "how can this be done." Only when you have exhausted every last "how" and find it still cannot be done is it truly impossible. The "cannot" at that point is a fact, and there is nothing dispiriting about admitting a fact. But when your first reaction to a thing is "I can't do it," that is often only an impression, and quite possibly nothing more than fear
