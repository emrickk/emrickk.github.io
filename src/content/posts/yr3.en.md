I still remember that day, the day I first went to school\
Back then every minute I lived had my parents at my side\
Then at last came a world I had to walk alone; the hands had to let go\
But I didn't want to let go, crying and looking back as I went

And then comes this day, the day I walk out into the world\
School long behind me; grown now, I should be long used to sailing without an escort\
Yet I stay childish: the moment something goes wrong, I want to go back\
To my parents' fortress, where no trouble can follow

* Don't go: even when the great bell rings, don't let go of my hand\
Once you are grown, nothing comes with a grown-up making way for you\
Don't go: out there in the crowd there will be grinding enough\
In the years ahead there will always be currents, with me and against me\
But right now, the care I am given, the selfless care, is not yet enough *

Be careful with everything: how dreary. Please stop\
Forcing me to be an adult; but the years are merciless, hurrying me to class to learn to walk alone\
Thousands of tons of responsibility, cold as a school motto; the tower clock keeps beating\
The classroom bell is ringing; why are my hands shaking
